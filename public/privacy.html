This Privacy Policy explains how [Your Legal Entity] (“Tavo,” “we,” “us”) collects, uses, and shares information when you use the Tavo point-of-sale platform (the “Service”). “Merchant” means a business using Tavo; “customer” means that business’s patrons.

1. Information we collect

  • Merchant account data — business name, contact details, staff names and login PIN hashes, settings.
  • Operational data — orders, payments (amounts, methods, tips, refunds), menu/products, inventory, shifts, reservations, and reports you generate.
  • Customer data you enter — names, phone numbers, emails, loyalty points, and order history for CRM, receipts, and marketing you choose to send.
  • Technical data — IP address, device/browser info, and logs used for security and reliability.

Card data: full payment-card numbers are handled by our certified payment processor(s) and the payment terminal — Tavo does not store full card numbers.

2. How we use information

  • To provide, secure, and improve the Service.
  • To process transactions and prevent fraud and abuse.
  • To send merchant account, service, and support communications.
  • To enable merchant-directed features such as digital receipts, loyalty, and email/SMS marketing — sent by the merchant to its own customers.

3. How we share information

  • Service providers — hosting, database, payment processors (e.g., Valor, Stripe), and messaging providers (email/SMS), acting on our behalf.
  • Between roles — merchant staff see their business’s data per their role; each merchant’s data is isolated from other merchants.
  • Legal — when required by law or to protect rights, safety, and security.
  • Business transfers — in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets.

We do not sell personal information, and we do not allow advertisers to pay to target Tavo users within the Service.

4. Roles & responsibilities

For customer data a merchant enters, the merchant is the controller and Tavo is a processor acting on the merchant’s instructions. Merchants are responsible for obtaining any needed consent (for example, before sending marketing messages) and for complying with laws applicable to their customers.

5. Data retention

We retain data while your account is active and as needed to provide the Service, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. You can request export or deletion of your business data as described below; some records may be retained where required by law.

6. Security

We use industry-standard safeguards: encrypted transport (HTTPS), hashed login PINs, role-based access, login rate-limiting, and keeping card data on certified payment hardware. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your data and to respond promptly to incidents.

7. Your choices & rights

Depending on where you and your customers are located, applicable rights may include access, correction, deletion, portability, and objecting to or restricting certain processing. Merchants can export or request deletion of their business data by contacting us. Customers should direct requests to the merchant they transacted with; we will assist the merchant as a processor.

8. International data

We may process and store data in the United States and other countries. Where required, we use appropriate safeguards for cross-border transfers.

9. Children

The Service is for businesses and is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age in your region). We do not knowingly collect data from children.

10. Changes

We may update this policy; material changes will be posted here with a new “Last updated” date and, where appropriate, additional notice.

11. Contact
